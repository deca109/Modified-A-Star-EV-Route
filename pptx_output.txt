Problem Statement
3
Given a road network represented as a graph G(V,E), where:
Nodes V represent intersections and charging stations 
Edges E represent road segments with associated distance, time, and energy cost 
And given:
Initial 
State of Charge (SoC)
 
Battery 
State of Health (
SoH
)
 
The goal is to 
find a feasible path from source to destination such that the EV never exhausts its battery 
and
 provide predictive maintenance information of the battery model.
Drawbacks / Challenges in Existing Works:
• Existing routing algorithms (A*, Dijkstra) ignore battery State-of-Health (
SoH
) and assume static conditions.
• No real-time integration of battery degradation (
SoH
) into routing decisions.
• ML-based methods (RL, GCN) are computationally expensive and require large training datasets.
• Metaheuristic approaches (GA, ACO) suffer from high computation time and poor scalability.
• Predictive maintenance is absent in all reviewed EV routing frameworks.
• No unified framework combining energy-aware routing, charging optimization, and battery health preservation.

--- Slide 4 ---
Motivations
4
Traditional vehicle navigation systems primarily optimize routes based on 
shortest distance or minimum travel time
, without considering critical energy-related factors — creating real-world challenges for the rapidly growing EV user base.
These systems:
Do 
not account for battery energy constraints
 during route planning 
Ignore 
battery State-of-Charge (SoC) / State-of-Health (SoC)
 variations across the journey 
Fail to consider 
battery degradation
 caused by driving patterns, terrain, and charging behavior 
As a result, users face:
Range anxiety
 due to uncertain battery sufficiency 
Frequent and inefficient charging stops
 
Reduced battery lifespan
 over time
Statistical Evidence:
AAA Survey (2022, n=1,051 U.S. adults): 60% cite charging infrastructure concerns, 58% worry about running out of charge, 55% find EVs unsuitable for long-distance travel — all routing-related barriers affecting over half of potential EV buyers.

Proposed Solution & Architecture
11
Core Techniques:
1. Spectral Clustering
Groups charging stations using 
geodesic distance
 
Forms topologically relevant clusters 
Reduces search space for routing
2. Fuzzy Reinforcement Learning
Applies 
Fuzzy Q-Learning
 to evaluate station clusters 
Inputs: traffic, waiting time, distance, SoC 
Outputs: desirability score for each cluster 
Helps in intelligent charging station selection
3. Modified A* Search
Extended A* with energy and battery-aware cost 
Cost function:  g(n) = distance + 
α⋅energy + β⋅(1 / 
SoH
 )
4. Battery Degradation Model
Based on Li-ion battery behavior 
Considers depth of discharge and usage cycles 
Dynamically updates 
State of Health (SoH)
 
Penalizes routes causing higher degradation
